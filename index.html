I am a doctoral researcher at the Faculty of Information Technology and Electrical Engineering (ITEE), University of Oulu, specialising in artificial intelligence and remote sensing for hydrology modelling. My work focuses on developing deep learning methods that extract snow and water characteristics from multi-modal satellite imagery — combining Sentinel-1 SAR, Sentinel-2 optical, and DEM data to build climate-resilient runoff prediction models.

My academic journey spans multiple continents: a BSc and MSc in electrical engineering and communication systems from Bahir Dar University, Ethiopia, followed by an Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degree (IPCV) across the University of Bordeaux (France), Autonomous University of Madrid (Spain), and Pázmány Péter Catholic University (Hungary). I am currently co-organising the HydroImaging Workshop at IEEE ICIP 2026 in Tampere, Finland — a testament to my commitment to the growing GeoAI community.

Beyond academia, I have worked as a Junior Electrical Engineer at the Ethiopian Electric Utility and Chief Technology Support at the American Space Ethiopia (U.S. Embassy), grounding my research in real-world engineering challenges and cross-cultural collaboration.

CrossFuse Active

Hybrid CNN-Transformer architecture for fusing Sentinel-1 SAR and Sentinel-2 optical satellite data to estimate high-resolution snow cover extent and snow water equivalent. The model integrates spectral band optimisation, attention mechanisms, and transfer learning to achieve superior performance on high-latitude, snow-dominated landscapes.

PyTorch Google Earth Engine Sentinel-1 SAR Sentinel-2 Transformers Python
GitHub
mDeepLabV3+ Published

Modified DeepLabV3+ architecture with ResNet-50/101 backbone for snow-cloud segmentation in Sentinel-2 multispectral imagery. Introduces dilated convolutions, optimised spectral band selection, and domain-adapted training to reliably separate snow cover from cloud cover — a persistent challenge in remote sensing.

TensorFlow OpenCV ResNet DeepLabV3+ Sentinel-2 Python
Paper (ESI)
Climate Social Media Monitor Published

Real-time NLP pipeline that mines social media streams to detect and monitor local climate impact events. Integrates transformer-based text classifiers with geo-tagged post analysis to identify floods, droughts, and extreme weather episodes as they unfold — bridging citizen science and AI for climate resilience.
